How to play chess:
King

The king can move one square in any direction.

Queen

The queen can move as many squares, horizontally, vertically or diagonally, provided no pieces are in the way.

Rook

The rook can move as many squares as needed, horizontally or vertically, provided no pieces are in the way.

Bishop

The bishop can move as many squares as needed diagonally, provided no pieces are in the way.

Knight

The knight moves in a "L-shape"; two squares horizontally or vertically, and one square in a perpendicular direction. The knight is unique in that it can jump over other pieces.

Pawn

The pawn can only move forwards one space, unless it has not been moved before, then it may move forwards two spaces. It captures one space diagonally in front of it, and there is a special move that can be used to capture enemy pawns if they move forwards two spaces called "En passant".
