How to play chess:

In general:

Chess is a two player game, where players take turns, with the objective of checkmating their opponent's king. Checkmate is defined as where the king is in check, and has no legal moves to get out of check.

King

The king can move one square in any direction. It may also castle with a rook, assuming neither the rook, nor the king have moved previously. You may not castle through check, into check or out of check. Check is defined as when the king can be captured by an opposing piece.

Queen

The queen can move as many squares, horizontally, vertically or diagonally as needed, provided no pieces are in the way.

Rook

The rook can move as many squares as needed, horizontally or vertically, provided no pieces are in the way. When castling with a king, the king takes two steps towards the rook, and the rook moves ajacent to the king, on the opposite side.

Bishop

The bishop can move as many squares as needed diagonally, provided no pieces are in the way.

Knight

The knight moves in a "L-shape"; two squares horizontally or vertically, and one square perpendicularly. The knight is unique in that it can jump over other pieces.

Pawn

The pawn can only move forwards one space, unless it has not been moved before, then it may move forwards two spaces. It captures one space diagonally in front of it, and there is a special move that can be used to capture enemy pawns if they move forwards two spaces called "En passant".
