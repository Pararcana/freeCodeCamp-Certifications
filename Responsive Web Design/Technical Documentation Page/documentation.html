How to Play


In general:

Chess is a two player game, where players take turns, with the objective of checkmating their opponent's king. The white player always starts first. The game ends when either checkmate has been achieved, timeout, or by agreement. Checkmate is defined as where the king is in check, and has no legal moves to get out of check. Check is defined as when the king is attacked by an opposing piece.

King

The king can move one square in any direction. It may also castle with a rook, assuming neither the rook, nor the king have moved previously. When castling, the king takes two steps towards the rook, and the rook moves ajacent to the king, on the opposite side. You may not castle through check, into check or out of check.

Queen

The queen can move as many squares, horizontally, vertically or diagonally as needed, provided no pieces are in the way.

Rook

The rook can move as many squares as needed, horizontally or vertically, provided no pieces are in the way.

Bishop

The bishop can move as many squares as needed diagonally, provided no pieces are in the way. Each bishop starts on one colour, and must stay on that colour throughout the game.

Knight

The knight moves in a "L-shape"; two squares horizontally or vertically, and one square perpendicularly. The knight is unique in that it is the only piece that can jump over other pieces.

Pawn

The pawn can only move forwards one space, unless it has not been moved before, then it may move forwards two spaces. It captures one space diagonally in front of it, and when it reaches the end of the board, it can be promoted to any piece that is not a king. There is also a special move, called "En Passant", which you may want to look up.

About the Board


Highlighting Squares

Right-click to highlight a square. Right-click the same square to bring it back to normal.

Drawing Arrows

Drag-right-click to draw an arrow between two squares. The "head" of the arrow will be on the square on which you released the mouse.

Moving Pieces

Click on the piece you want to move, then click on the legal square you want to move it to. Alternatively, you can click and drag to move pieces.
